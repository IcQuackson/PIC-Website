Prototype development now has a clearer target than "build something that works." The proposal and pitch materials already lock in the hard constraints: a self-powered UAV relay payload, fast boot-to-mesh behavior, local voice and telemetry services, and enough instrumentation to measure recovery after a partition.

That means any change to the bill of materials should follow evidence from integration tests. If a component stays, it should be because it helps meet payload, runtime, or link-quality targets, not because it looked convenient on paper.
